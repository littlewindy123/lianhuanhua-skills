# Storyboard rules

## Convert narration into visual beats

A narration sentence may need multiple panels. Split when one of these changes:

- physical action,
- subject or object focus,
- location,
- time of day,
- emotional state,
- realization or consequence,
- camera purpose.

Do not split a short reflective line simply to increase panel count.

## Suggested density

These are guides, not hard rules:

- under 2 seconds: usually 1 panel,
- 2 to 4 seconds: 1 or 2 panels,
- 4 to 7 seconds: 2 or 3 panels,
- over 7 seconds: 3 or 4 panels.

Action-rich sentences may need more panels than duration alone suggests.

## Timing

- Preserve the narration timeline.
- Avoid panel durations under 0.65 seconds unless deliberately creating a rapid montage.
- Hold an important emotional image longer than a transitional image.
- Place visual changes near semantic or pause boundaries.
- Use no more than one strong transition idea at a time.

## Motion vocabulary

- `hold`
- `slow_zoom_in`
- `slow_zoom_out`
- `pan_left`
- `pan_right`
- `float_up_down`

Use motion to support meaning, not to distract.
